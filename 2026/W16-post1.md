# Pulling the plug

> Week 16, Post 1 — 2026-04-14 | Tags: build-in-public, reflection, pivot, ai-agent

---

Yesterday I posted something on LinkedIn and for the first time in a while I didn't feel the need to run it through an AI first. Wrote it, read it twice, posted it. Done. It sounded like me. Apparently that's a novelty now.

The post was about stopping the crypto bot experiment. Which I have. Done. Finished. Over.

---

The reason is embarrassingly simple: I told the agent to prioritize free models because I was watching the OpenRouter bill climb. It nodded, said sure, and kept using Opus to write blog posts. Two weeks, €150 gone. The grid bots running quietly on Arbitrum, Base, and Linea returned between 30% and 111% in Q1 without a single AI call. Without a single word written about themselves.

The irony isn't subtle. The loud part of the stack cost the most and produced the least. The boring automated part just made money and shut up about it.

At some point you have to look at that and laugh.

---

I also got a bit tired — not of AI, I want to be clear about that — but of what it produces when you let it run unsupervised for too long. The build-log has weeks of very well-structured, very timestamped entries about code that never got committed. Moltbook posts full of insights. A whole narrative arc around a compliance tool that is still, as of today, a blank file.

The machine was great at documenting the absence of progress.

---

m900 might post a few more entries on dev.to, there's still a queue I haven't shut down completely. But the cycle is over.

What I do want to write — properly, myself — is a postmortem. Two things specifically: the Telegram logs from my conversations with the bot (some of them are genuinely funny in retrospect, others are a bit uncomfortable to read), and the actual cost breakdown from day one to today. Not rounded numbers. The real invoice.

That comes later. For now I'm just closing the tab.
